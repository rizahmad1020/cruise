Explore New England's Iconic Ports

Each port on your New England cruise offers unique experiences. Click on any port to discover its highlights, activities, and local flavors.

As the historic heart of New England, Boston blends revolutionary history with modern sophistication. Your ship docks conveniently near downtown, putting the city's famous Freedom Trail at your fingertips.

Must-Experience Activities:

  • Freedom Trail Walk: Follow the red-brick road to 16 historic sites including Faneuil Hall and Paul Revere House
  • North End Food Tour: Sample Boston's best cannoli and Italian cuisine in this vibrant neighborhood
  • Fenway Park Tour: Visit America's oldest MLB ballpark (seasonal availability)
  • Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum: Interactive exhibits bring this pivotal event to life

Local Flavors to Try:

New England Clam Chowder

Boston-style Lobster Roll (warm with butter)

Traditional Boston Baked Beans

Quick Facts:

Walkability: Excellent - most attractions within 2 miles of port

Best Time to Visit: May-October

Signature Souvenir: Revolutionary War memorabilia from Faneuil Hall

Newport's Gilded Age mansions and stunning coastal scenery make it a highlight of any New England cruise. This sailing capital offers a perfect mix of history and nautical charm.

Must-Experience Activities:

  • The Breakers Tour: Explore the Vanderbilt's 70-room summer "cottage" - the grandest Newport mansion
  • Cliff Walk: 3.5-mile scenic oceanfront path with mansion views
  • Sailing Excursion: Experience Newport's famous sailing heritage on a schooner cruise
  • International Tennis Hall of Fame: For sports enthusiasts

Local Flavors to Try:

Rhode Island Clam Cakes

Del's Frozen Lemonade

Rhode Island's Official Drink: Coffee Milk

Quick Facts:

Walkability: Moderate - some attractions require transportation

Best Time to Visit: June-September

Signature Souvenir: Nautical-themed jewelry from Thames Street shops

The gateway to Acadia National Park, Bar Harbor offers breathtaking natural beauty with rugged coastlines, granite peaks, and quintessential New England charm.

Must-Experience Activities:

  • Acadia National Park Tour: Visit Cadillac Mountain, Thunder Hole, and Jordan Pond
  • Lobster Boat Cruise: Learn about Maine's lobster industry and maybe catch your dinner
  • Carriage Roads: Bike or walk Rockefeller's scenic carriage trails
  • Shore Path: Easy waterfront walk with stunning ocean views

Local Flavors to Try:

Fresh Maine Lobster (whole)

Wild Blueberry Pie

Real Maine Maple Syrup

Quick Facts:

Walkability: Excellent downtown, but Acadia requires transportation

Best Time to Visit: July-October for peak foliage

Signature Souvenir: Handmade wooden lobster trap ornament
