Sail Along the Scenic Pacific Coast

Experience the magic of the Pacific coastline with its rugged cliffs, charming coastal towns, and breathtaking sunsets. Our Pacific Coast cruises offer the perfect blend of relaxation and adventure, with opportunities to explore vibrant cities like San Diego, San Francisco, and Seattle, as well as hidden gems along the way.

Featured Ports of Call

San Diego, California

Explore the historic Gaslamp Quarter, visit the world-famous San Diego Zoo, or relax on Coronado Beach with its iconic Hotel del Coronado.

  • Balboa Park museums
  • USS Midway Museum
  • La Jolla sea caves

Santa Barbara, California

Known as the "American Riviera," Santa Barbara offers Spanish colonial architecture, excellent wine tasting, and beautiful beaches.

  • Stearns Wharf
  • Mission Santa Barbara
  • Funk Zone art district

San Francisco, California

The iconic Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, cable cars, and Fisherman's Wharf make this one of America's most beloved cities.

  • Golden Gate Park
  • Chinatown
  • Muir Woods

Astoria, Oregon

Where the Columbia River meets the Pacific, this historic town offers maritime museums, Victorian homes, and stunning coastal views.

  • Columbia River Maritime Museum
  • Astoria Column
  • Fort Stevens State Park

Wildlife & Nature Experiences

The Pacific Coast is home to some of North America's most spectacular wildlife and natural wonders. From migrating whales to ancient redwoods, our cruises offer unparalleled opportunities to connect with nature.

Most Popular

Whale Watching Adventures

Witness the incredible migration of gray whales (December-April) or spot humpbacks and orcas (May-November). Our naturalist guides will help you spot these magnificent creatures and explain their behaviors.

  • Best viewing spots: Monterey Bay, Channel Islands, Juan de Fuca Strait
  • Onboard spotting scopes and expert commentary
  • Guaranteed whale sightings or receive a future cruise credit
Learn About Excursions

Redwood Forest Exploration

Walk among giants in the ancient coastal redwood forests. Our excursions to Muir Woods or Humboldt Redwoods State Park include guided walks and photography tips.

Trees over 2,000 years old Easy to moderate trails

Tide Pool Discoveries

Explore vibrant intertidal ecosystems at low tide. See sea stars, anemones, and other marine life up close with our marine biologist guides.

Best at morning low tides Great for families

Seal & Sea Lion Colonies

Visit bustling sea mammal rookeries at places like La Jolla or Pier 39 in San Francisco. Hear their distinctive calls and watch their playful antics.

Prime viewing year-round Pup season in spring

Coastal Bird Watching

The Pacific Flyway attracts millions of migratory birds. Spot pelicans, cormorants, puffins, and maybe even the endangered California condor.

Bring your binoculars Bird checklist provided
